A Dutch passport holder who grew up in India, eats Bengali, reads Victorian novels, cooks South Asian food for European guests, and has now visited 45+ countries and 30+ UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Wageningen is home.

Interests & Life Outside Work

What occupies the rest of the mind.

📚

Classic Literature

My reading list has a heavy Victorian bias. Dickens for social observation that still bites, Austen for prose precision that most academics will never achieve. I read both as fieldwork manuals for understanding how institutions and people interact — not, I admit, the intended use. I am also partial to Tagore (in the original Bengali, which feels like home) and the occasional detour into Russian novels when the Wageningen winters require it.

🍛

Indian Cooking

I cook. Seriously. Bengali cuisine — its mustard-forward fish curries, its obsession with bitter vegetables, its complete disregard for chilli heat in favour of complexity — is something I reproduce in a Wageningen kitchen that probably never expected to smell like this. I also cook across the South Asian spectrum, which has been both a social asset and a way of maintaining continuity with a country I left but have not left. Guests are warned in advance.

✈️

Travel — 45+ Countries

The job has taken me to places maps mark in beige ("conflict zone") and places the travel industry marks in gold. I have eaten chapati in a South Sudanese seed bank, been delayed by cattle on a Myanmar road, and attended a seed fair in Somaliland. Outside work, I have visited 45+ countries with my family, and I approach each one as an anthropologist — curious about food, public spaces, and what people argue about.

🏛

UNESCO World Heritage Sites — 30+

I have developed an inexplicable personal project of visiting UNESCO World Heritage Sites — currently at 30+. Not as a checklist exercise, but because the UNESCO list, with all its politics and gaps, is one of humanity's more interesting attempts to say: this matters, remember it. Some of the best have been the least famous. Some of the most famous have been the least moving. I have strong opinions about which is which.

🧬

Intellectual Curiosity — Across Fields

I am interested in how ideas move between disciplines. My Substack essay "Error as Engine" grew from a side-reading of Kimura's neutral theory of molecular evolution — which I encountered while thinking about AI hallucination rates. I find that seed systems, information systems, and literary narrative have more structural similarities than either biologists or literary critics would comfortably admit. This is mostly a feature, occasionally a social liability.

Family & Life in Wageningen

I live in Wageningen with my family. My daughter attends VWO Gymnasium and intends to study psychiatry — a choice I find both impressive and inexplicable, in the best way. Wageningen is an unusual place to live: a small town that houses one of the world's leading agricultural universities, which means it is simultaneously very international and very specialised. I have adapted to Dutch directness, Dutch cycling, and Dutch weather. Not necessarily in that order of difficulty.
